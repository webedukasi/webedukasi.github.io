2. In the sentences below replace "which" with "that." When you have done this, re-write all the sentences without either "which" or "that." The meaning remains the same but the sentences become less formal.

Original sentences:

1. The book, which I borrowed from the library, is very interesting.

2. The car, which is parked outside, belongs to my neighbor.

3. The house, which has a blue door, is for sale.

4. The cat, which is black and white, is very playful.

5. The movie, which was released last week, received positive reviews.

Sentences with "that":

1. The book that I borrowed from the library is very interesting.

2. The car that is parked outside belongs to my neighbor.

3. The house that has a blue door is for sale.

4. The cat that is black and white is very playful.

5. The movie that was released last week received positive reviews.

Sentences without "which" or "that":

1. The book I borrowed from the library is very interesting.

2. The car parked outside belongs to my neighbor.

3. The house with a blue door is for sale.

4. The black and white cat is very playful.

5. The movie released last week received positive reviews.

By removing "which" or "that," the sentences become less formal but still convey the same meaning.
