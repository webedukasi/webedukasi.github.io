2. Exercise 1 Answer the following questions based on the story of "Chinese Dragon". Why did the man want to live forever? What did the man know about the dragon? What did the man want from the dragon? Why did people in the island buy poison? What happened to the man after the dragon take him to the island? Why did the man want the dragon take him back? What can we learn from the story? Give a title to the story! 1. 2. 3. 4. 5. 6. 7. 8.​


Jawaban:

1. Why did the man want to live forever?

Answer: The man wanted to live forever because he desired eternal life and believed it would bring him happiness and fulfillment.

2. What did the man know about the dragon?

Answer: The man knew that the dragon possessed the ability to grant immortality.

3. What did the man want from the dragon?

Answer: The man wanted the dragon to grant him immortality so that he could live forever.

4. Why did people on the island buy poison?

Answer: The people on the island bought poison because they wanted to kill themselves. They believed that death was preferable to living forever, as they saw the man's eternal existence as a curse.

5. What happened to the man after the dragon took him to the island?

Answer: After the dragon took him to the island, the man realized the loneliness and despair of living forever. He witnessed the people on the island choosing death over immortality and realized the consequences of his desire.

6. Why did the man want the dragon to take him back?

Answer: The man wanted the dragon to take him back because he realized the folly of his wish for eternal life. He wanted to return to his mortal existence and live a finite but meaningful life.

7. What can we learn from the story?

Answer: We can learn that the pursuit of immortality without considering the consequences can lead to feelings of loneliness, despair, and a lack of purpose. The story highlights the importance of appreciating the transient nature of life and finding value in the limited time we have.

8. Give a title to the story!

Answer: "Desire for Immortality: The Lonely Journey of a Man and the Wisdom of Mortality"
