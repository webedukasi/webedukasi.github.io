22. Eli wants to see object in front of her eyes in 25 cm. If the distance of the retina is 2,5 cm, the focus of retina is


So, the focus of eye lens is 2,27 cm.

Explanation :

Our eyes have the lens principle. It because, our eyes have a convex lens. Convex lens ini our eyes is at our eye lens. Like other convex lens, our eye lens has positive focus. So, let's we know first the formula to calculate about a lens focus.

[tex] \boxed{\bold {\frac{1}{f}=\frac{1}{s} + \frac{1}{s'}}}[/tex]

If :

f = the focus of lens (cm)s = distance object from lens (cm) s' = distance shadow/image from lens (cm) Work Steps :

We know that :

s = distance object from lens = 25 cm s' = distance shadow/image from lens = 2,5 cm

Question : f = the focus = ... cm

Answer :

[tex] \frac{1}{f}=\frac{1}{s} + \frac{1}{s'}[/tex]

[tex] \frac{1}{f}=\frac{1}{25} + \frac{1}{2,5}[/tex]

[tex] \frac{1}{f}=\frac{1}{25} + \frac{10}{25}[/tex]

[tex] \frac{1}{f}=\frac{11}{25}[/tex]

[tex] 11f = 25[/tex]

[tex] f = \frac{25}{11} = \boxed{2,27 \: cm}[/tex]
