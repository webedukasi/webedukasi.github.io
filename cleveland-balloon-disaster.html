9. artikel bahasa inggris tentang natural disaster


An earthquake is what happens when two blocks of the earth suddenly slip past one another. The surface where they slip is called the fault or fault plane. The location below the earth’s surface where the earthquake starts is called the hypocenter, and the location directly above it on the surface of the earth is called the epicenter.

Sometimes an earthquake has foreshocks. These are smaller earthquakes that happen in the same place as the larger earthquake that follows. Scientists can’t tell that an earthquake is a foreshock until the larger earthquake happens. The largest, main earthquake is called the mainshock. Mainshocks always have aftershocks that follow. These are smaller earthquakes that occur afterwards in the same place as the mainshock. Depending on the size of the mainshock, aftershocks can continue for weeks, months, and even years after the mainshock!

flood

The rainy season that hit the country made several areas that had been raining for days affected by floods. The overflowing river water and the inability of the culverts and sewers because they are clogged with garbage have caused floods that have not subsided to this day. In addition to flooding, hilly areas also experienced landslides so that many roads were forced to be closed for fear of endangering the people who crossed the road.

Based on the narrative of a student who had just returned to his hometown, heavy rains that did not stop caused him and his family to pack important and valuable items as supplies and were forced to move because their house was flooded. They were forced to be moved by disaster rescue forces using canoes and bots. They are grateful that the villagers were evacuated safely. The residents felt sad because they saw that their entire village had been submerged in water. They can only pray that the flood that drowned their house will subside soon so that they can return to their respective homes and carry out their normal activities.

While in the refugee camps, they were provided with various necessities by the government and donors such as food, water, medicine, and clothing. Mothers and girls together cook food. However, the children seemed bored because they could not freely play. The shelter provided is very narrow and can only be used for sleeping. Water supply and latrine services are also inadequate, so many children are sick with diarrhea because the refugee camps are not clean. The head of the refugee group has submitted a request for health and hygiene services but the government has not yet responded to it.

During the week of experiencing a flood disaster and living in a refugee camp, the situation finally recovered. After a week, the floodwaters receded. The residents were happy to be able to return to their homes even though the conditions were so dirty with garbage and mud. Many possessions such as carriages, livestock and plants were destroyed. Finally they rushed to clean their house with the help of water spray from the local cleaning service. The flood disaster that hit their village left bitter memories for all the villagers. They prayed that this incident would not happen again and that the government would strengthen the embankments and flood prevention facilities.

Based on data obtained from the National Disaster Management Agency (BNPB), national losses due to the disaster averaged around Rp. 30 trillion. In just five days, floods, landslides, and tornadoes hit 20 districts/cities in Indonesia. Temporary data from BNPB, 10 people died, 4 people were missing, 2 were injured, 100 houses were damaged, and hundreds of houses were flooded.

The human factor is more likely to cause flooding. The public's lack of concern for the environment by littering and irresponsible felling of trees are classic factors that will not go away. Prevention has been carried out by the government by building control DAMs in the upper reaches of the river and infiltration so that the water does not fall too quickly at the same time to the mainland. The water in the control dam must be intensively incorporated into the ground. However, improvement and development must also be balanced with the community's concern for nature itself. The environment is a part of our life. So, it is fitting for us to help keep our environment clean. Waste handling and recycling should be encouraged. In addition, tree planting must also be carried out and cared for
