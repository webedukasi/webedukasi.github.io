21. What is definition of caption


CAPTIONS

The definition of caption:

Caption is brief description, heading, or title that identify or introduce a document, graphic, photograph, or table. It is consist of additional information which available in the pictures/photos. Usually it’s located below the picture itself.  

PEMBAHASAN:

The purpose of the caption is to help readers or viewers to understand more information that might not be in photos or videos.

The Structure of Caption  

The title  The lead  Section Heading  

The Language Features  

Exclamation  Question  Adjective Phrase  Personal Phrase  

Rule of Writing a caption  

Supply specific information (Answering 5W1H Question) Use present tense on the first sentence  Caption are written in a complete sentences  Clearly identify the people and location in the photo  Identify the person from left to right  

Kinds of Caption  

Identification bar: Jenis caption ini hanya menyebutkan siapa yang berada di gambar Cutline: Jenis caption ini menyebutkan siapa yang berada digambar dan apa yang dilakukannya  Summary: Berisi penjelasan lengkap tentang siapa yang berada digambar, apa yang dilakukan, kapan kejadiannya, dimana tempat terjadinya, dan kenapa hal tersebut dilakukan.  Expanded: Bentuk lebih lengkap dari summary, jenis caption ini juga memuat bagaimana kejadiannya dan kutipan.  Group identification: Sama seperti jenis caption identification bar, hanya saja jenis ini digunakan apabila gambar memuat lebih dari satu orang.  Quote: Berisi kutipan perkataan seseorang yang berhubungan dengan gambar.

34. 1.What is caption? 2. What is the importance of caption? 3. What are the characteristics of a good caption? 4. What are types of caption? 5. How to make a good caption? 6. What is the text structure of a caption? 7. Please make a conclusion of a caption!​


Jawaban:

1. Caption refers to a short description or text that accompanies an image, photograph, or illustration. It provides context, explanation, or additional information to enhance the viewer's understanding or appreciation of the visual content.

2. The importance of a caption lies in its ability to complement and enhance the visual content it accompanies. Captions can serve several purposes, such as providing necessary details, clarifying the subject matter, evoking emotions, or delivering key messages. They help capture the viewer's attention and create a more engaging and informative experience.

3. Characteristic of a good caption includes:

- Conciseness: Captions should be brief and to the point, conveying the essential information without being overly wordy.

- Relevance: A good caption should be directly related to the visual content and provide relevant context or details.

- Clarity: Captions should be clear and easily understood, avoiding overly technical or ambiguous language.

- Engaging: An effective caption captures attention and engages the viewer by being interesting, creative, or thought-provoking.

- Accuracy: Captions must accurately represent the visual content and provide correct information.

4. There are different types of captions, including:

- Descriptive Captions: Focus on providing factual information about the visual content, such as identifying subjects, locations, or events.

- Explanatory Captions: Offer explanations or interpretations of the visual content, helping viewers understand the context or meaning.

- Narrative Captions: Tell a story or present a sequence of events related to the visual content.

- Humorous or Creative Captions: Use humor, wit, or creativity to add an entertaining or distinctive element to the visual content.

5. To create a good caption, you can follow these steps:

- Understand the visual content: Analyze the image or illustration to grasp its key elements, message, or mood.

- Identify the purpose: Determine what information or emotion you want to convey through the caption.

- Keep it concise: Craft a brief but impactful caption that gets straight to the point.

- Be descriptive and relevant: Provide relevant details, explain the context, or highlight the main subject of the visual content.

- Be creative (if appropriate): Use humor, puns, or creativity to make the caption more engaging, but ensure it remains relevant and respectful.

6. The text structure of a caption is usually concise and straightforward. It can vary depending on the content and purpose but often follows a simple format of introducing the subject, providing additional information or context, and concluding with a clear message or takeaway.

7. In conclusion, a caption serves as an important complement to visual content by providing context, information, or emotional cues. A good caption is concise, relevant, engaging, accurate, and enhances the viewer's understanding or appreciation of the visual content. It should capture attention, convey the intended message, and make the overall experience more enjoyable and informative.
