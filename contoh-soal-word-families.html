3. Apa pengertian dari word families ?


Kelompok kata (word families) adalah kelompok kata dengan arti (meanings) dan pengucapan (spellings) yang mirip. Setiap kata dalam kelompok berbeda dalam part of speech-nya. Sebagai contoh: agreement adalah kata benda, agreeableadalah kata sifat, to agree adalah kata kerja.

20. :How does migration affect families and Communities : Bagaimana migrasi mempengaruhi keluarga dan Komunitas


Migration can have both positive and negative effects on families and communities. Here are some ways in which migration can impact them:
1. Economic Impact: Migration can lead to improved economic conditions for families and communities. When individuals migrate to areas with better job opportunities, they can send remittances back to their families, contributing to their financial well-being. Additionally, migrants may bring new skills and knowledge, leading to economic growth and development in their destination communities.
2. Family Separation: Migration often involves leaving family members behind in search of better opportunities. This can result in emotional and psychological stress for both migrants and their families. The distance can strain relationships and impact the upbringing and support of children, as well as the overall dynamics within the family.
3. Social and Cultural Changes: Migration can bring cultural diversity and exchange of ideas, enriching the social fabric of communities. However, it can also create cultural tensions, as different cultural norms and traditions intermingle. This can lead to both integration and challenges in terms of social cohesion.
4. Brain Drain or Gain: Migration can result in a “brain drain” in the origin communities, where skilled individuals leave for better opportunities elsewhere. This can create a shortage of professionals who are necessary for community development. On the other hand, migrants may acquire new skills and knowledge abroad, and upon return, contribute to the development of their communities through entrepreneurship, investment, or knowledge transfer.
5. Healthcare and Social Services: Migration can strain the healthcare and social service systems in both origin and destination communities. Migrants may require access to healthcare, education, and social welfare services, which can put a strain on the resources of communities hosting them. Similarly, origin communities may face challenges in providing services for those left behind, such as children or elderly family members.
It is important to note that the impact of migration on families and communities can vary depending on the context, policies, and support systems in place. Understanding these dynamics is crucial for addressing the challenges and harnessing the potential benefits of migration.

22. what are ant families called?​


Ants are eusocial insects of the family Formicidae and, along with the related wasps and bees, belong to the order Hymenoptera. Ants appear in the fossil record across the globe in considerable diversity during the latest Early Cretaceous and early Late Cretaceous, suggesting an earlier origin. Ants evolved from vespoid wasp ancestors in the Cretaceous period, and diversified after the rise of flowering plants. More than 13,800 of an estimated total of 22,000 species have been classified. They are easily identified by their geniculate (elbowed) antennae and the distinctive node-like structure that forms their slender waists.
