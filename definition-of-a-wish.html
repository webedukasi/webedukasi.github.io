. What type of word is wishes?

As detailed above, 'wish' can be a verb or a noun. Here are some examples of its usage: Verb usage: We wish you a Merry Christmas.

. What tense is used after I wish?

To refer to a past wish, use a past perfect verb after the object. To form the past perfect, use had followed by a past participle verb. She wishes she had written down the phone number. I wish I had known that you were in town last week.

. Is I wish I were there grammatically correct?

"I wish I was there" means that the speaker wishes (using the present tense: “wish”) that he or she had been (past tense: “was”) at a specific place at a past time. "I wish I were there" means that the speaker wishes (using the subjunctive mood) that he or she is (present tense) at a specific place at the present time.

34. definition of responding of suggestion​


Jawaban:

The term "responding to suggestion" refers to the act of reacting, answering, or acknowledging a proposal, idea, or recommendation that has been put forward by someone else. It involves providing feedback, feedback, or an action in response to the suggestion made by another person or a group.

When someone responds to a suggestion, they may do so in various ways, including:

1. Acknowledging the suggestion: Expressing gratitude or recognition for the suggestion put forward.

2. Accepting the suggestion: Agreeing with the proposed idea or recommendation and indicating a willingness to implement it.

3. Declining the suggestion: Politely expressing that the suggestion is not feasible or suitable in the given context.

4. Providing feedback: Offering constructive feedback on the suggestion, whether it's positive or requires improvement.

5. Explaining the decision: Giving reasons for either accepting or rejecting the suggestion to provide clarity to the person who made it.

6. Taking action: Implementing the suggested idea or recommendation in practice.

Responding to suggestions is an essential aspect of effective communication and collaboration, as it fosters open dialogue and allows for the exchange of ideas and perspectives. It can lead to improvement, innovation, and problem-solving in various settings, such as businesses, organizations, or personal interactions.


35. what is the definition of hope and wish


WISH AND HOPE DIFFERENCE
 
Wish and Hope are believed to be two words that have the same meaning. Though both differ on context.

HOPE
 
In contrast to wish, hope is a hope that can be realized and very likely to happen.

Example:

I hope we can still be friends.

The equivalent of I hope is I can be I hope ... I can ...

WISH
 
It is a difficult hope to realize even impossible.

Example:

I wish I can fly.

The equivalent of I wish is I could be I wish ... I could ...

* Could here not be in the past tense but rather express something unreal or unlike.
